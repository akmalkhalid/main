Mohd Nor Akmal Khalid (Ts. Dr.)

Greeting! I am a senior lecturer at the Faculty of Information Science and Technology, Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia (UKM) and also a member of the Center for Artificial Intelligence Technology (CAIT). I had previously served as an assistant professor in the School of Information Science, Japan Advance Institute of Science and Technology (JAIST) and a part-time Research Fellow in the School of Computer Sciences, Universiti Sains Malaysia (USM). I have obtained my graduate degrees from the University of Science Malaysia since 2018. My work focuses specifically in the techniques used for optimization and artificial intelligence technology in the fields of operational research and game informatics. My topics of interest, include but are not limited to, intelligent and evolutionary search, AI in games, simulation and modeling, and decision support system. I'm looking into harmonizing games and AI in diverse settings. More on that later!

Projects

  • Human-Machine Collaborative Framework Using Generative Adversarial Network and Map-Elites for a Creativity-Based Learning (2024--2026)

    A new frontier in artificial intelligence (AI) that focused on mediating human-machine collaboration for creativity-based learning is investigated. It emphasizes the use of search-based procedural content generation (PCG) technologies, like Map-Elites, alongside generative adversarial networks (GANs). Current collaboration systems lack personalization and adaptability due to limited autonomy and data. A meta-learning technique is proposed to address these challenges, enabling effective autonomy with limited data. The research aims to develop a co-dependent system based on GANs and Map-Elites, automate the collaboration framework using meta-learning, and design an evaluation scheme to maximize the framework's performance. The methodology includes three phases: developing the framework, automating it with meta-learning, and designing a new evaluation scheme. The project's outcome aims to create an innovative creativity-based learning framework, potentially transforming education and knowledge acquisition. The significance lies in enhancing social dynamics, education quality, and workforce autonomy, while reducing training and knowledge transfer costs.

  • Fusion of Data-Driven and Theory-Driven Research: Towards Empathic Entertainment Field (2022--2023)

    The integration of AI technology and Entertainment Science has revolutionized gaming experiences, particularly in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic. This collaboration has become increasingly vital across various industries. The project's goal is to establish empathetic entertainment as a robust research field that can provide insights into human interactions and behaviors.

  • Establishing A New Research Field of “Comfort in Mind” Science and Technology (2021--2022)

    The integration of AI technology in gaming has brought about significant advancements in research areas such as game-solving and general game-playing (GGP). However, there is a need for more empirical verification of the concept of "motion-in-mind" due to its subjective nature. While AI can assist in identifying enjoyable aspects of games, it still lacks the ability to comprehensively assess levels of distress and break down complex events with imperfect information. To address these limitations, this project aims to combine AI research and entertainment science to establish a new field of study called "comfort in mind" through games.

  • Determining the Effects of Global Warming to Depression Patients in Malaysia using Hybrid of Deep Recurrent Neural Network and Hidden Markov Model Approach (2019--2022)

    The World Health Organization reported that the number of people affected by depression has increased in recent years. However, one of the contributing factors that are least investigated is global warming, which has raised the hospital admission rate of depression patients. This study explores the impact of global warming concerning age group and residency on the upswing rate of depression patient hospital admission. The study's objectives are to formulate the relationship of global warming, place of residence, and age to the increasing rate of depression patients, determine the ambient temperature that impacts depression patients adversely, and propose and evaluate a hybrid of DRNN-HMM approach to predict the rising rate of depression patients. The expected implication of the study is a state-of-the-art machine learning approach that can formulate and alert the impact of global warming on depression patients in Malaysia.
